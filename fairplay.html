Linking music schools to business schools, pairing the musicians to the students clubs and associations that host networking cocktails monthly. When applying for college, choosing to pursue a career in music is a real gamble. You have no guaranteed income stream when you get your diploma, instead constantly having to market yourself in order to find enough gigs to make a living, and even if you do score some, it’s often a hassle to get paid promptly. Musicians don’t have the time or skills to look for gigs or afford a booking agent. Currently, people looking for musicians use mostly word of mouth and don’t have a great variety of choices of local musicians. Also, too often, musicians don’t get paid on time and have very little recourse for bad debt. There is a mismatch in supply and demand for orchestra positions and meanwhile orchestras have a hard time filling up seats. Musicians are limited to the people they can play with because it is hard to advertise for many bands at the same time. Music school spend on having an Entrepreneurial Musicianship departement that only serves few while many struggle with debt after their studies. If only there was a solution that would allow musicians to land more gigs and have more artistic freedom. If only people looking for musicians had a great variety of available local musicians. If only universities would have access to an efficient tool that would help all their music students not only the top few.
